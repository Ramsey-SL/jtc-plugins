# Journey Mapping Framework Reference

Scoring rubrics, stage definitions, and analysis templates for structured customer journey intelligence.

---

## Journey Stage Definitions

### Stage 1: Discovery / First Touch

The moment a potential customer first encounters the brand. Evaluate the quality of that first impression across all entry points.

**Evaluation Criteria**:

| Criterion | Weight | What to Look For |
|-----------|--------|-----------------|
| Landing page clarity | 25% | Value prop visible above fold, clear what the brand sells |
| Visual first impression | 20% | Professional design, brand consistency, not cluttered |
| Load speed / performance | 15% | Page loads quickly, no layout shifts, images optimized |
| Navigation discoverability | 20% | Can you immediately find what you need? |
| Mobile experience | 20% | Responsive, touch-friendly, no horizontal scroll |

**Scoring Guide**:
- **9-10**: Instantly clear, professional, compelling. You want to explore further.
- **7-8**: Good first impression, easy to understand, minor friction.
- **5-6**: Acceptable but unremarkable. Some confusion about what they offer.
- **3-4**: Cluttered, slow, or confusing. Value prop unclear.
- **1-2**: Broken, unprofessional, or actively repulsive. You'd leave immediately.

---

### Stage 2: Browse / Explore

How the user moves through the site to find products or information.

**Evaluation Criteria**:

| Criterion | Weight | What to Look For |
|-----------|--------|-----------------|
| Category structure | 25% | Logical hierarchy, not too deep, clear labels |
| Search quality | 20% | Relevant results, autocomplete, typo tolerance |
| Filtering / sorting | 20% | Useful filters, responsive, mobile-friendly |
| Content richness | 15% | Buying guides, videos, blog content, fit guides |
| Cross-sell / discovery | 20% | "Related", "You may also like", collections |

**Scoring Guide**:
- **9-10**: Effortless browsing. Powerful search. Rich filtering. Excellent content.
- **7-8**: Easy to browse. Good category structure. Decent search.
- **5-6**: Functional but bare. Limited filters. Basic search.
- **3-4**: Hard to find products. Poor or no search. Shallow categories.
- **1-2**: Broken navigation. No search. Dead ends.

---

### Stage 3: Evaluate / Compare

How well the site helps a user make a purchase decision.

**Evaluation Criteria**:

| Criterion | Weight | What to Look For |
|-----------|--------|-----------------|
| Product page depth | 25% | Multiple images, detailed specs, descriptions, videos |
| Reviews / social proof | 20% | Star ratings, review count, review quality, photos |
| Fitment / compatibility | 15% | Year/make/model lookup, compatibility info, fitment guarantee |
| Trust signals | 20% | Warranty, returns policy, security badges, guarantees |
| Comparison capability | 20% | Compare tool, alternatives shown, buying guides |

**Scoring Guide**:
- **9-10**: Product pages are mini-experiences. Reviews are abundant and helpful. Easy to verify fit.
- **7-8**: Good product info. Reviews present. Trust signals visible.
- **5-6**: Basic product pages. Some reviews. Standard trust signals.
- **3-4**: Thin product pages. Few or no reviews. Hard to trust.
- **1-2**: No product details. No reviews. No trust signals.

---

### Stage 4: Convert / Purchase

The add-to-cart through order-confirmation experience.

**Evaluation Criteria**:

| Criterion | Weight | What to Look For |
|-----------|--------|-----------------|
| Cart experience | 20% | Clear summary, easy to edit, upsells not intrusive |
| Checkout flow | 25% | Number of steps, guest checkout, progress indicator |
| Payment options | 20% | Credit card, PayPal, Apple/Google Pay, BNPL (Affirm, Klarna) |
| Shipping transparency | 20% | Cost shown early, delivery estimates, options |
| Conversion optimization | 15% | Exit intent, cart recovery, urgency (real, not fake) |

**Scoring Guide**:
- **9-10**: Seamless 1-2 step checkout. Multiple payment options. Real-time shipping.
- **7-8**: Smooth checkout. Good payment options. Clear shipping info.
- **5-6**: Standard checkout. Basic payment. Shipping info could be clearer.
- **3-4**: Clunky checkout. Limited payment. Shipping surprises.
- **1-2**: Broken or painful checkout. Forces account creation. Hidden costs.

---

### Stage 5: Post-Purchase

Everything between order confirmation and the customer having the product in hand.

**Evaluation Criteria**:

| Criterion | Weight | What to Look For |
|-----------|--------|-----------------|
| Confirmation experience | 20% | Clear order summary, tracking info, timeline |
| Shipping updates | 25% | Proactive updates, tracking quality, carrier integration |
| Packaging quality | 15% | Branded packaging, protection, inserts, unboxing experience |
| Support access | 25% | Easy to reach, multiple channels, response speed |
| Returns process | 15% | Clear policy, easy process, prepaid labels |

---

### Stage 6: Retention / Loyalty

How the brand maintains the relationship after initial purchase.

**Evaluation Criteria**:

| Criterion | Weight | What to Look For |
|-----------|--------|-----------------|
| Email strategy | 25% | Frequency, relevance, personalization, design quality |
| Loyalty program | 25% | Points, tiers, rewards, exclusivity, engagement |
| Community building | 20% | Forums, social groups, events, ambassador programs |
| Remarketing quality | 15% | Ad frequency, relevance, not annoying |
| Repeat purchase UX | 15% | Saved info, reorder, recommendations, wishlists |

## UX Pattern Library

Common patterns to look for and document:

### Navigation Patterns
- Mega menu with images
- Sticky header with search
- Year/Make/Model selector (automotive)
- Breadcrumb navigation
- Recently viewed products bar

### Product Page Patterns
- Image gallery with zoom
- 360-degree product view
- Video on product page
- Tabbed specs/description/reviews
- "Complete the look" bundles
- Fitment verification widget

### Conversion Patterns
- Sticky add-to-cart button
- Quick-view / quick-add
- Progress bar checkout
- One-click reorder
- Abandoned cart email sequence

### Trust Patterns
- Review aggregate in header
- "As seen in" media logos
- Security badges at checkout
- Satisfaction guarantee badge
- Live chat widget

### Engagement Patterns
- Email capture pop-up (timing, offer)
- SMS opt-in
- Wishlist / save for later
- Price drop alerts
- Back-in-stock notifications
